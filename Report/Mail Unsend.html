Practical Example

Imagine you sent an email to your client containing incorrect pricing details. Realizing the mistake moments later, you need to act fast. Platforms like Gmail provide an Undo Send feature, giving you a limited time to prevent the delivery.

How It Works: After clicking send, Gmail shows an "Undo" option at the bottom left. This feature allows you to stop the email for 5 to 30 seconds based on your settings.

Steps to Unsend an Email:

  1. Immediately after sending, look for the "Undo" notification.
  2. Click "Undo" to stop the email delivery.
  3. The email will reopen, allowing you to correct errors.
  4. If the "Undo" option disappears, the email is delivered.
Pro Tip: Ensure the Undo Send feature is enabled in Gmail. Go to Settings > General > Undo Send and adjust the cancellation window.
